Safety Rules

Cycling rules and regulations in The Hague, like in the rest of the Netherlands, are designed to

ensure the safety of cyclists and other road users while promoting efficient and sustainable

transportation.

Here are some key cycling rules and regulations in The Hague:

Cyclists in The Hague must obey the same traffic rules as motorized vehicles.

Whenever there is a dedicated bicycle lane or path, cyclists are generally required to use it.

If you're cycling in the dark or in conditions with reduced visibility, your bicycle must have working lights.

Park your bicycle in designated areas or bike racks.

Use hand signals to indicate turns and stops.

Remember that local regulations and enforcement may vary slightly, so it's a good idea to familiarize yourself with the specific rules in The Hague by checking with local authorities or consulting local resources. Safe and considerate cycling not only ensures your safety but also contributes

to the overall cycling culture in the city.
